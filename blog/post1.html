Blog / The Inaugural Post
Published 11th of April, 2019
Why didn’t I just use Medium…

Hello there, Internet Cosmonaut! This is my blog. My very own space on the Internet.

Now, I’m not too sure what people usually blog about, but I can say that this is my very first blog post. To celebrate, I’m going to tell the story behind this website and how it shaped the design of this site.

A Tale of Frustrations

Since 2017, I had been trying to build, and maintain my own website. Everytime I start a project, I like to have a rough idea of the end product. For me, at the time, I decided I wanted a site with a Material Design UI. So, I decided on the now-depreciated Material Design Lite, a bunch of css components staright from Google to make your site as “Material-ly” as possible.

So, I started with the header aaaaaaand, 3 days later I was still struggling with implementing javascript and dealing with CSS classes. I got to the “Ah, screw it” point about a few hours into the 3rd day.


3 Days of misery and crying myself to sleep got me this.

After a few months I decided to start on a fresh slate, this time using Bootstrap CSS, so I could learn a Marketable skill while making my website. I was learning everything fine and dandy, Until I got to grids & columns.

Ho. Ly. Shit. Never In my life had I wanted to pull my hair out more.

I had to learn responsive design AND responsive design for older IE versions! I said screw it. I’ll go back to lurking in The Android custom ROM scene & PCMasterRace Community.

And thus, began my 9 month “coding” (HTML isn’t a real language, fite me!) Hiatus.

Reddit & My evolution

During the 9 month hiatus, I explored more and more of Reddit. I witnessed:

  • The Snap (Although I did not participate)
  • The evolution of the meme economy
  • The rise of wholesome memes
  • The switch to the (horrible imo) redesign

But what I also discovered was /r/ProgrammerHumor. And yesterday, I saw this post. Reading through the comments, this comment lead me to discover enough.css. And honestly, the pitch was enough to get me to try. I mean, think about it:

  • 458 bytes minified CSS
  • Meaning that, assuming another 500 bytes for my custom CSS (Color scheme, additional padding)
  • And another 7 kilobytes of webpage content
  • A TOTAL PAGE SIZE OF 8000 BYTES!

8 KILOBYTES!

I could read 125 pages and only use up 1 MEGABYTE! I can’t really express how significant this is other than saying counting ads, a news site (even optimized for mobile) could easily use up 2MB just for 1 page! 2 MEGABYTES!

Side note: I may have also been biased towards it due to my taste for minimalism

An evening gone…

So, with newfound motivation, I started on the site this evening. Within 15 minutes I had the basic layout, and after 5 more minutes of W3Schools and StackOverflow, the additional CSS. To be honest the content was the hardest part, taking up about 1.5 hours to complete, due to my lack of creativity. Theeeeen, I decided I wanted a blog aswell. So, I spent another half an hour on the blog.

The end?

No. So remember like 4 sentences ago I said I lacked creativity? Yea. I ended up spending about 1.5 hours just writing this blog post.

But I still published it the same day, with 1 hour to spare.

Fin.

Thank you for reading my first blog post.
