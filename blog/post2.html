Blog / 9 Months Empty.
Published 11th of January, 2020
Everything’s new, except for the style.

As the title might have suggested, it has been 9 months since my last blog post. While no one reads this (or maybe you do, I have no analytics!), I feel angry with myself for not having done much over the 9 months. I could just put deflect it with a couple of semi-valid excuses, but that would be cheating and not a great way to start off a year (and decade!). So instead, let’s look back, reflect and think forward for the future.

What’s different now?

Irrelavant:

  • I feel even more empty

  • We’re in 2020!

  • I’ve got my second monitor now

  • AMD & Intel have resolutely swapped positions in terms of CPUs

  • Zen 2 is out now

  • I’ve managed to convert 2 people from Windows to Linux

  • Arch-ified a long time Ubuntu user

  • Android Q is out (I refuse to acknowledge the new name of Android 10)

Relavant:

  • The keyboard on my Lenovo 100s diedðŸ˜ 

  • This site is now statically generated with pandoc

Yes, it may look the same, but now, instead of being manually written line by line, the site is automagically assembled together by pandoc, allowing for flexibility and modularity. While currently I have just reimplemented the previous theme and layout, this reimplementation will allow me to easy change/update the site’s look and feel without too much effort, as I only have to modify the template I build from now.

Looking back at my Github, I opened Issue: rebase to JAMstack on the 4th of July, 2019. Yet, it took until the 10th of January, 2020 for me to deploy the new site. 7 months, it took me to git commit to my site again, even though it only took the last 48 hours to build, test and deploy this new pandoc generated site. Which, while irrelavant to you, dear reader, makes me tremendously upset with myself for some unknown reason.

A quick side note: While reading through my first blog post I cringed a bit at what I wrote. While I have personally reflected on what I have said back then, I will not share that here, neither will I delete my first blog post.

Looking forward

That said, I can’t change the past, so going forward, I will try my best to write more blog posts and most importantly, write more code.

In 2 days time, I will be collecting my national exam results and applying for tertiary education. While I cannot say I am happy with my self-perceived performance during the exams, I can say I’m OK with what I’ve done. Maybe reality will make or break me, maybe I won’t feel much, but I think it’s important to have a current snapshot of my mental state.

I have some downtime between now and when I start schooling again, and I hope during this period I can write a couple of blog posts and more importantly, start (and hopefully finish) a couple of personal projects.

Fin.

Here’s to a good 2020.
